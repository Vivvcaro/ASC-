I am a researcher and consultant working at the intersection of neuroscience, immersive sound, and altered states of consciousness. My work focuses on how experiences can be designed — not just facilitated — to guide perception, emotion, and awareness.

I am one of the few practitioners working across immersive sound design, consciousness research, neuroaesthetics, sonic art, and ritual studies. This allows me to approach altered states not only as experiences, but as systems that can be structured, refined, and scaled.

My practice integrates scientific research with an artistic approach rooted in deep listening, minimalism, and contemporary ritual. I draw on the lineage of Pauline Oliveros, the teachings of Plum Village (founded by Zen Master Thich Nhat Hanh), and Carl Jung's active imagination.

In parallel, I compose and perform using field recordings, modular synthesis and AI, blending natural and electronic textures to create immersive soundscapes designed for inner exploration, communal attunement, and altered perceptual states.

I am particularly interested in how these states — awe, absorption, deep presence, shared emotional resonance — enhance imagination, creativity, intuition, and a sense of agency. In an era increasingly shaped by artificial intelligence, I see these capacities as essential.

My work ultimately asks: how can we design experiences that expand human perception — safely, intentionally, and meaningfully?

Research

The Architecture of Acoustic
States of Consciousness

Altered states of consciousness are not anomalies — they are core to human cognition, creativity, and transformation. My research investigates how these states can be intentionally designed through sound, space, and attention. Drawing from neuroscience, ritual studies, and music, I explore how immersive environments modulate perception, reduce cognitive rigidity, deepen emotional processing, and facilitate self-transcendent experience.

01 — Science

Neuroaesthetics
& Psychology

Default mode network suppression during deep listening, psychoacoustic triggers for flow and awe states, and the neuroscience of beauty as a doorway to expanded awareness.

02 — Foundations

Quantum
Mechanics

Exploring quantum coherence in biological systems and its implications for consciousness. The Observer Effect as a framework for intentional listening — how the act of witnessing changes the field you inhabit.

03 — Tradition

Ritual
Research

Cross-cultural documentation of sound-induced altered states: Shipibo icaros, Mongolian khoomei, Tuvan throat singing, and West African drumming. Forty thousand years of human ceremony as evidence.

04 — Composition

Musicology &
Electronic Music

From Pauline Oliveros's Deep Listening to today's immersive electronic composers. How spectral composition and frequency architecture create the conditions for expanded inner states.

05 — Environment

Immersive
Sound

Spatial audio design, resonant frequency physics, and the architecture of listening environments. How binaural recording, speaker placement, and acoustic treatment shape the depth of the inner journey.

06 — Frontier

Psychedelic
Research

The role of music in Johns Hopkins and Imperial College psilocybin trials. Non-pharmacological protocols as standalone and complementary practices at the intersection of sound, medicine, and transformation.

What I do

Designing the right Conditions for
Transcendental Experiences

Transcendental experiences are not accidents. They arise from a precise architecture of sound, space, time, and intention — when each element is placed with care, the conditions for transformation become reliable.

I design these conditions. Working with retreat centres, clinics, festivals, and cultural organisations, I develop sonic protocols and experiential frameworks that guide individuals and groups into structured states of expanded awareness — supporting emotional processing, perceptual shifts, awe, and deep integration.

This is not about adding music to an experience. It is about engineering how consciousness moves through time.
