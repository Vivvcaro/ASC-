Acoustic States
of Consciousness

What if music is not just an entry point into altered states, but a training ground for them?

What if, before entering psychedelic experiences, we could train perception, attention, and ego flexibility through carefully designed sonic environments? Not to relax. Not to escape. But to learn how to navigate consciousness itself.

What this is

A research framework

Acoustic States of Consciousness explores how sound can shape perception, behaviour, emotion, and cognition. Not as art or as therapy, but as a technology for expanding consciousness.

Why it matters

We are entering a new era of consciousness exploration.

Psychedelics, neurotechnology, and mental health interventions are gaining momentum — yet most approaches share a limitation: they tend to prioritise induction over preparation.

We amplify the mind, without training it.

Sound offers a different entry point.

  • It is non-invasive
  • It is accessible
  • It is already deeply embedded in human experience
  • And it operates at a systems level in the brain

From dopaminergic reward pathways to memory encoding, from neural entrainment to motor activation — sound reshapes the structure of experience in real time.

The approach

Set & Setting

Acoustic States of Consciousness is a structured pathway into altered states through sound. Grounded in neuroscience and informed by music theory and lived experience, my approach focuses on:

A subtle, carefully designed priming process that builds perceptual readiness before any deeper immersion.

Not everyone responds to these experiences in the same way. The framework accounts for difference — in history, sensitivity, and readiness — rather than flattening it.

Immersive sonic systems that expose participants to novelty, intensity, ambiguity, and perceptual shifts. Not to relax, but to build stability within the unknown.

Shifting from uncontrolled immersion to intentional exploration, where curiosity replaces fear as the stabilising force.

Viviana Caro — Research & Sonic Practice

Thanks for visiting my page.

I'm Viviana Caro; a polymath, researcher, and consultant working at the intersection of neuroscience, immersive sound, and altered states of consciousness.

My work explores how experiences can be intentionally designed, not merely facilitated, to shape perception, modulate emotion, and expand awareness. I approach altered states not as ephemeral phenomena, but as structured systems that can be composed, refined, and responsibly scaled.

Operating across immersive sound design, consciousness research, neuroaesthetics, sonic art, and ritual studies, I bring a rare interdisciplinary perspective to the field. This allows me to bridge scientific rigour with artistic sensibility, developing frameworks for experiences that are both experientially profound and methodologically grounded.

My practice integrates empirical research with an aesthetic language rooted in deep listening, minimalism, and contemporary ritual. It draws on the lineage of Pauline Oliveros, the contemplative teachings of Plum Village, and Carl Jung's concept of active imagination, recontextualised for modern, technologically mediated environments.

In parallel, I compose and perform using field recordings and modular synthesis, crafting immersive sonic environments that blend organic and synthetic textures. These works are designed to facilitate inner exploration, collective attunement, and shifts in perceptual states.

I am particularly interested in how states such as awe, absorption, deep presence, and shared emotional resonance can enhance imagination, creativity, intuition, and agency. In an era increasingly shaped by artificial intelligence, I see these human capacities not as peripheral, but as essential.

At its core, my work is an invitation to engage with a post-materialist approach that blends spirituality, art, science, and technology into a unified framework for exploring and expanding human perception.

Research

The Architecture of Acoustic
States of Consciousness

Altered states of consciousness are not anomalies — they are core to human cognition, creativity, and transformation. My research investigates how these states can be intentionally designed through sound, space, and attention. Drawing from neuroscience, ritual studies, and music, I explore how immersive environments modulate perception, reduce cognitive rigidity, deepen emotional processing, and facilitate self-transcendent experience.

Immersive Lab — University of Greenwich

Neuroaesthetics
& Psychology

Default mode network suppression during deep listening, psychoacoustic triggers for flow and awe states, and the neuroscience of beauty as a doorway to expanded awareness.

Quantum
Mechanics

Exploring quantum coherence in biological systems and its implications for consciousness. The Observer Effect as a framework for intentional listening — how the act of witnessing changes the field you inhabit.

Ritual
Research

Cross-cultural documentation of sound-induced altered states: Shipibo icaros, Mongolian khoomei, Tuvan throat singing, and West African drumming. Forty thousand years of human ceremony as evidence.

Musicology &
Electronic Music

From Pauline Oliveros's Deep Listening to today's immersive electronic composers. How spectral composition and frequency architecture create the conditions for expanded inner states.

Immersive
Sound

Spatial audio design, resonant frequency physics, and the architecture of listening environments. How binaural recording, speaker placement, and acoustic treatment shape the depth of the inner journey.

Psychedelic
Research

The role of music in Johns Hopkins and Imperial College psilocybin trials. Non-pharmacological protocols as standalone and complementary practices at the intersection of sound, medicine, and transformation.

What I do

I Research and Design
Transcendental Experiences

Transcendental experiences are not accidents. They arise from a precise architecture of sound, space, time, and intention — when each element is placed with care, the conditions for transformation become reliable.

I design these conditions. Working with retreat centres, clinics, festivals, and cultural organisations, I develop sonic protocols and experiential frameworks that guide individuals and groups into structured states of expanded awareness — supporting emotional processing, perceptual shifts, awe, and deep integration.

This is not about adding music to an experience. It is about engineering how consciousness moves through time.
